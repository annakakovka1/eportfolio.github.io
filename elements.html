Final Reflection: Critical Evaluation of Machine Learning Learning and Professional Development

Overview

This reflection critically evaluates my development across the machine learning module, focusing on algorithmic understanding, individual contribution, teamwork experience, and professional growth. It demonstrates how my perspective has evolved from technical execution to critical, ethical, and context-aware machine learning practice.

Understanding of Machine Learning Algorithms

At the beginning of the module, I approached machine learning primarily as a technical process focused on implementing algorithms. However, through activities such as the perceptron tasks, Jaccard similarity analysis, model evaluation experiments, and CNN coursework, I developed a more critical understanding of algorithm suitability and limitations.

The perceptron exercises demonstrated that model effectiveness depends heavily on dataset structure. For instance, the perceptron successfully classified the AND dataset due to its linear separability, but this highlighted its inability to model non-linear relationships (Rosenblatt, 1958). Extending this to multi-layer perceptrons revealed how hidden layers increase representational capacity while introducing risks such as overfitting and increased complexity (Goodfellow, Bengio and Courville, 2016).

The Jaccard coefficient activity further strengthened my critical thinking. While it provided a clear similarity metric, it oversimplified relationships by treating all variables equally. This revealed that quantitative outputs must be interpreted carefully, particularly in sensitive domains such as healthcare, where ethical and legal considerations, including GDPR compliance, are essential (European Union, 2016).

Through the model performance task, I learned that evaluation metrics such as AUC and R² must be interpreted in context. Adjusting regularisation parameters showed how model performance is shaped by design choices, reinforcing that metrics alone do not guarantee generalisation (James et al., 2021).

The CNN activity highlighted the strengths of deep learning in feature extraction while also exposing limitations in interpretability and real-world reliability. This aligned with concerns about bias and opacity in AI systems (Bender et al., 2021). Overall, my understanding has shifted from implementation to critical evaluation.

Individual Contribution to Team Activities

My main contribution to the Airbnb project was in refining the research question and strengthening the analytical direction. I helped shift the focus from a general pricing analysis to a more structured investigation of optimal pricing across neighbourhood and room-type segments.

I also contributed to identifying methodological limitations, particularly the use of availability as a proxy for booking likelihood. By raising these concerns, I supported a more transparent and realistic interpretation of the data. This reflects a higher level of contribution, where the focus is not only on completing tasks but improving analytical quality (Provost and Fawcett, 2013).

Initially, I felt uncertain about challenging group decisions. However, as the project progressed, I became more confident in contributing critical perspectives supported by evidence. This development improved both my analytical contribution and my engagement within the team.

Experience as a Team Member

Working within a team highlighted the importance of balancing technical, analytical, and business perspectives. Early differences in approach—between rapid model development and careful problem definition—created some tension. However, these differences ultimately improved the quality of the project by encouraging deeper analysis.

This experience aligns with collaborative data science practices, where interdisciplinary thinking enhances outcomes (Kuhn and Johnson, 2019). I developed stronger communication skills, particularly in explaining technical ideas clearly, and improved my ability to engage constructively with different viewpoints.

I also recognised the importance of accountability and consistent participation. Meeting deadlines, responding to feedback, and contributing reliably were essential to maintaining team effectiveness.

Professional and Personal Development

This module significantly influenced my professional perspective. I now understand that machine learning practice extends beyond technical implementation to include ethical responsibility, transparency, and critical judgement.

Discussions on AI risks, such as bias and misinformation, reinforced the importance of responsible design and governance (Bender et al., 2021). Similarly, legal frameworks such as GDPR emphasise accountability in handling data (European Union, 2016). These insights have shaped my understanding of machine learning as a socio-technical discipline.

On a personal level, I have developed greater confidence in critical thinking and self-reflection. I now focus not only on achieving results but on understanding their validity and implications. This represents a significant shift in my learning approach.

Learning and Future Application

The most important outcome of this module is my ability to critically evaluate machine learning systems. I now consider problem definition, dataset suitability, evaluation validity, and ethical implications as integral components of analysis.

In future work, I would apply more rigorous validation techniques, such as cross-validation, and place greater emphasis on explainability. I would also engage more proactively in early-stage discussions to improve project direction.

This module has prepared me to approach machine learning not only as a technical discipline but as a professional practice requiring responsibility, judgement, and continuous reflection.

Final Insight

The most significant development in my learning is the shift from focusing on model performance to evaluating the entire analytical process. Effective machine learning is not defined by accuracy alone, but by the quality of reasoning, ethical awareness, and professional responsibility applied throughout.
